Design Portfolio

Hi, there!

That's me, Liz. I originally hail from Chicago, but live in Oslo, Norway. I hold a Bachelor of Science in Psychology and a Master of Arts in Design. I've spent the last 8 years across various design sectors from interiors to web to graphic to UX. I enjoy creating human experiences, both in the three and two dimensional worlds. My latest interests surround design systems; building, maintaing, measuring and driving adoption. Design [eco]systems require attention to detail, visual acuity, and cross-functional work; areas I thrive in.

On my journey learning about design systems I found myself pursuing more frontend knowledge as I became increasingly curious on the how to implement just as much as the what. I enjoy the nitty-gritty challenges where function meets form and love being part of the solutions that make the designs come to fruition.

What I'm up to now

Currently, I am the Lead UX-UI Designer for a tech startup based in Norway. We are building a developer platform that enables companies to integrate with their legacy systems so they can leverage existing data into new business logic and applications.


In addition to leading the design work and strategy for our platform, I am helping lead the development and adoption of our design system along with two developers. We are currently building our design system, using MUI components as our base library with custom components on top. I am driving the design, maintainance, adoption and documentation of the system while collaborating closely with our developers responsible for building and scaling the library.


Because our design system lives and breathes in Storybook, which is a frontend workshop, with a code repository in Github, my work is becoming increasingly intertwined with these developer tools.


More about this portfolio

This abridged portfolio is the result of my efforts to learn more about those tools, including Visual Studio Code, Github and HTML/CSS so I can work more seamlessly with our developers and design system. To create this portfolio I downloaded a template from BootstrapMade, used Visual Studio Code to modify the HTML and CSS to meet my design needs, integrated Github with VSC to learn more about CI/CD workflows and ultimately deploy this project to Github.
